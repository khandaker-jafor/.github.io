I am an interdisciplinary postdoctoral research fellow and ecologist at the Smithsonian Institution in Washington, DC. My work sits at the intersection of climate change, population dynamics, and public health, with a particular focus on vulnerable communities in low- and middle-income countries.

My current research evaluates the health co-benefits of Nature-based Solutions through watershed interventions, alongside broader questions about how extreme weather events and environmental stressors shape fertility, migration, mortality, and human wellbeing. I bring together mixed-methods research, demographic analysis, and geospatial tools to understand vulnerability and inform climate adaptation policy.

## Current roles

I serve as a **Chapter Scientist for the IPCC Seventh Assessment Report (AR7), Working Group II, Chapter 20** on "Poverty, Livelihoods, Mobility and Fragility", contributing to the global scientific assessment of climate impacts on vulnerable populations. I am also affiliated as a research fellow with several institutions advancing climate and health research in South Asia and East Africa.

## Research interests

My research spans several connected themes:

- **Extreme weather, climate change, and climate vulnerability** in exposed regions
- **Population dynamics** — fertility, migration, and mortality under environmental stress
- **Health in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs)**, including climate-health linkages
- **Disaster risk reduction (DRR)** and community resilience
- **Mixed-methods research** combining quantitative, qualitative, and spatial approaches

## Background

I hold a Ph.D. in Environmental Studies from the University of Adelaide (2022), and a Master's and Bachelor's in Sociology from Shahjalal University of Science and Technology, Bangladesh. Before joining the Smithsonian, I held postdoctoral fellowships at the University of Michigan and Georgetown University, working on climate, health, and migration surveillance across Bangladesh, Kenya, and Uganda.

I have published widely on the links between climate, demography, and health, and I serve as an editor and reviewer for a range of journals in population, environment, and public health. You can find my full list of publications, talks, and teaching through the links on this site, or browse my work on [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=PS_CX0AAAAAJ).
