As a postdoctoral research fellow at Georgetown University's Institute for the Study of International Migration, I worked on the Bangladesh Environment and Migration Survey (BEMS), a large household survey examining environmental change, livelihoods, and migration in south-western Bangladesh.

The research explores how salinity, environmental stress, and livelihood transitions shape migration decisions, in-situ adaptation, and intergenerational outcomes.

**Role:** Postdoctoral Research Fellow
**Affiliation:** Institute for the Study of International Migration, Georgetown University
**Themes:** Environmental migration, adaptation, livelihoods, survey research
