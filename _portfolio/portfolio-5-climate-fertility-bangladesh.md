Spanning my doctoral and postdoctoral work, this research programme examines how extreme weather events and climate vulnerability shape fertility preferences, intentions, and outcomes among households in flood- and cyclone-prone regions of Bangladesh.

Drawing on household surveys, demographic data, and a climate vulnerability index framework, the work has produced a sustained body of peer-reviewed publications on the climate-fertility nexus, child mortality perceptions, and population dynamics under environmental stress.

**Role:** Lead researcher
**Themes:** Climate-fertility nexus, demography, climate vulnerability, Bangladesh
