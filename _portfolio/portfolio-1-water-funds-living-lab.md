This project evaluates the health co-benefits of Nature-based Solutions (NbS) delivered through watershed interventions. As an interdisciplinary postdoctoral research fellow at the Smithsonian Institution, I lead the design and evaluation of how upstream watershed restoration and land-management interventions translate into measurable downstream benefits for human health and wellbeing.

The work combines a causal-pathway framework linking biophysical, psychosocial, and One Health pathways to priority health outcomes, with a quasi-experimental evaluation design and household survey instruments. Methods draw on panel survey data, geospatial analysis, and mixed-methods fieldwork.

**Role:** Lead postdoctoral researcher
**Affiliation:** Smithsonian Institution
**Themes:** Nature-based Solutions, environmental health, watershed management, programme evaluation
