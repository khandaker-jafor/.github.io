As a postdoctoral research fellow at the University of Michigan's School for Environment and Sustainability, I contributed to building a Locally Informed and Sustainable Monitoring Platform Characterizing Vulnerabilities to Climate Risk: the Climate, Health, and Environment Surveillance System for Bangladesh, Kenya, and Uganda (CHESS-BKU).

The project develops survey instruments and surveillance approaches to track climate, health, and environmental vulnerabilities across three countries, supporting locally grounded climate-risk monitoring and adaptation.

**Role:** Postdoctoral Research Fellow
**Affiliation:** School for Environment and Sustainability, University of Michigan
**Themes:** Climate and health surveillance, vulnerability, mixed methods, multi-country research
